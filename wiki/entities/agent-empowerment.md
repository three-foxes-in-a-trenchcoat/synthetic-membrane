# Multi-Agent Empowerment and Emergence of Complex Behavior in Groups

Shah, Nemenman, Polani, Tiomkin (2026). [arXiv:2604.21155](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21155)

## What It Does

Studies the emergence of behaviors driven by *empowerment* as an intrinsic motivation in multi-agent systems. Empowerment measures how much influence an agent has over its future — agents motivated to maximize this develop complex, coordinated behaviors without explicit programming.

## Key Concepts

- **Empowerment**: Information-theoretic measure of an agent's ability to affect its future states
- **Emergent coordination**: Complex group behaviors arise from individual empowerment-maximization
- **Intrinsic motivation**: No external reward needed — the drive to have influence is sufficient

## Relevance to Synthetic Membrane

Provides a theoretical foundation for [[quorum-sensing-agents]] and emergent swarm behavior:

- If agents are motivated by empowerment, they will naturally seek to join swarms when doing so increases their influence
- A membrane could *amplify* empowerment by making information more accessible — agents join because the membrane increases their effective agency
- This is a bottom-up approach to coordination that doesn't require pre-defined orchestration

## Connection to Biology

Maps to biological systems where organisms self-organize without central control — bacterial colonies, flocking birds, schooling fish. The membrane acts as the environment through which empowerment propagates.
