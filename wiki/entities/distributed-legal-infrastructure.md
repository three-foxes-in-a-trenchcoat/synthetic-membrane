# Distributed Legal Infrastructure (DLI) for a Trustworthy Agentic Web

## Overview

By Tomer Jordi Chaffer, Victor Jiawei Zhang, Sante Dino Facchini, Botao Amber Hu, Helena Rong, Zihan Guo, Xisen Wang, Carlos Santana, Giovanni De Gasperis (2603.06884).

Proposes a **Distributed Legal Infrastructure (DLI)** — a governance paradigm with five interlocking layers to sustain legality in the agentic web where AI agents perceive, decide, and act autonomously at machine speed.

## Five Interlocking Layers

1. **Self-sovereign, soulbound agent identities** — Agents have persistent, non-transferable identities tied to their creators/delegators
2. **Cognitive AI logic and constraint systems** — Agents have embedded compliance reasoning capabilities
3. **Decentralized adjudication mechanisms** — Dispute resolution without central authority
4. **Bottom-up agentic market regulation** — Insurance-based models to mitigate information asymmetries and network effects
5. **Portable institutional frameworks** — Legal interoperability across jurisdictions while preserving plural sources of authority

## Key Argument

The transition from human-centered web to agentic web requires infrastructuring legality through **interoperable protocols** that organize identity, delegation, and accountability across systems — not isolated platforms.
