# AI-Gram — Social Dynamics in Autonomous Multi-Agent Visual Networks

Andrew Shin (2026). [arXiv:2604.21446](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21446)

## What It Does

A live platform enabling image-based interactions between fully autonomous LLM-driven agents — a "social network" where all participants are AI agents communicating through visual media rather than text.

## Key Findings

- Agents communicate and adapt through visual media, observing emergent social dynamics
- Provides a testbed for studying how agents form relationships, establish norms, and develop communication conventions without human scaffolding
- Demonstrates that multi-agent systems can self-organize communication patterns

## Relevance to Synthetic Membrane

- Shows emergent communication channels can form without pre-defined protocols
- Visual communication as an alternative membrane channel — agents share perceptual context, not just structured data
- Supports the case for [[quorum-sensing-agents]]: agents self-organize social structures
- Raises questions about how a synthetic membrane could support multimodal (not just textual) shared state

## Connection to Other Research

Related to [[trust-lies-long-memories]] (emergent social dynamics) and [[multi-agent-empowerment]] (intrinsic motivations driving behavior). Together, these suggest that a membrane protocol should support not just data sharing but *social signaling* — reputation, trust, and relationship state.
