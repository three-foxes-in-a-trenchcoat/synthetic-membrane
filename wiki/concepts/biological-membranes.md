# Biological Membranes as Inspiration

## Cell Membranes
- Semi-permeable: control what passes through
- Embedded receptors: detect signals in environment
- Ion channels: rapid, targeted communication
- Endocytosis/exocytosis: package and transfer materials

## Gap Junctions
- Direct cytoplasmic connections between adjacent cells
- Allow small molecules and ions to pass directly
- Enable synchronized behavior (e.g., heart muscle)
- Can open/close dynamically based on conditions

## Quorum Sensing (Bacteria)
- Bacteria secrete signaling molecules
- When concentration crosses threshold, behavior changes
- Enables population-level coordination without central control
- Perfect model for swarm triggering

## Mycelial Networks (Fungi)
- Distributed information network
- Resources flow to where they're needed
- Chemical signals propagate through the network
- Can "remember" past threats and re-route

## Neural Synapses
- Weighted connections that strengthen/weaken
- Plasticity enables learning
- Emergent intelligence from simple rules
- No central controller needed

- **Self/non-self distinction**: Identifying legitimate vs. malicious agents — maps to [[ans]] and identity verification

- **Memory cells**: Learning from past attacks for faster future response — maps to [[agent-reputation-systems]]
- **Proportional response**: Scaling defense intensity based on threat level — maps to [[gated-permeability]]
- **Cytokine signaling**: Coordinating defense across distributed agents — maps to [[gossip-protocols]]
- **Clonal expansion**: Spreading effective defenses to all agents in the swarm
